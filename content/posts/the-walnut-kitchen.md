There is a particular pleasure in a kitchen that has been thought through to the last drawer pull. This one — wrapped, floor to ceiling, in vertical-grain American walnut — manages the rare trick of feeling at once monumental and entirely usable.

## A Material That Improves with Time

The cabinetry was built by a single shop upstate, joinery first, hardware second. Brass pulls, unlacquered, are already darkening in pleasing patches where hands habitually fall.

## The Counter as Stage

A slab of honed Calacatta runs the full length of the room — no seams, no fuss — and accepts the daily liturgy of coffee, tomatoes, school lunches without complaint.
